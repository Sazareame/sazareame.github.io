This chapter describes a basic shading model for an opaque surface illuminated by a point light source.

Point-like Light Sources

The point-like light sources come in two flavours:

A point source is small enough to be treated as a point, but is close to the scene and can illuminate different surfaces differently. e.g. flashlight

A direction source is also small enough to be treated ad point-like and also so far away that it illuminates all surfaces the same and there is no need to keep track of its location, only its direction. e.g. the sun.

Basic Reflection Models

Lambertian Reflection

The simplest kind of reflection is a surface that reflects light equally to all directions, regradless of where it came from. This kind of reflected light can be represneted by a constant multiple of the irradiance: A surface that behaves this way is known as an ideal diffuse surface.

Its colour is view independent and is compltely described by its reflectance, The reflectance can be different for different colours of light.

Specular Reflection
