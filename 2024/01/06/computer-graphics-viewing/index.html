Viewing Transformations

The viewing transformation has the job of mapping 3D locations, represented as coordinates in the canonical coordinate system, to coordinates in the image, express in units of pixels.

Many graphics systems do this by using a sequence of three transformations:

A camara transformation or eye transformation, which is a rigid body (刚体) transformation that places the camera at the origin in a convenient orientation. It depends only on the pose (the position and orientation) of the camera.

A projection transformation, which projects points from camera space so that all visible points fall in the range -1 to 1 in and . It depends only on the type of projection desired.

A viewport transformation or windowing transformation, which maps this unit image rectangle to the desired rectangle in pixel coordinates. It depends only on the size and position of the output image. We say that the camera transformation converts points in canonical coordinates (or world space) to camera coordintes or place them in camera space. The projection transformation moves points from camera space to the canonical view volume. Finally, the viewport transformation maps the canonical view volume to screen space.
